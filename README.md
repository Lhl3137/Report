# Oilfield Well Site Safety Assessment Report

## I. Overall Overview

This assessment evaluates the safety compliance of an oilfield well site based on remote sensing data analysis. The site contains 7 detected facilities: 3 buildings (IDs 0, 2, 6), 1 storage tank (ID 1), and 3 oil wells (IDs 3, 4, 5). The analysis combines quantitative distance measurements with visual interpretation to identify potential fire safety hazards according to national and industry standards.

## II. Quantitative Metrics Interpretation

**Detection Results Summary:**
- Total facilities detected: 7
- Ground Sampling Distance (GSD): 0.2 m/pixel
- Building facilities: 3 units (IDs 0, 2, 6)
- Storage tank: 1 unit (ID 1)
- Oil wells (beam pumping units): 3 units (IDs 3, 4, 5)

**Critical Distance Measurements:**
- Building 0 to Tank 1: **9.67m** (VIOLATION)
- Tank 1 to Building 2: **10.52m** (VIOLATION)
- Building 0 to Building 2: 0.0m (adjacent structures, compliant per standards)
- Minimum oil well spacing: 21.54m (between IDs 3 and 4)

## III. Risk Matrix and Violation Analysis

**Identified Safety Violations:**

1. **Building 0 and Storage Tank 1 (9.67m spacing)**
   - **Violation:** Distance significantly below required minimum
   - **Standard Reference:** SY/T 5225-2019 Section 3.1.3(c) - "Power house and tank area, natural gas storage and processing facilities shall be no less than 20m apart"
   - **Risk Level:** HIGH - Critical fire safety hazard

2. **Storage Tank 1 and Building 2 (10.52m spacing)**
   - **Violation:** Distance 47% below required minimum
   - **Standard Reference:** SY/T 5225-2019 Section 3.1.3(c) - Same requirement as above
   - **Risk Level:** HIGH - Critical fire safety hazard

**Risk Matrix Hotspots:**
- The tank (ID 1) shows violations with two adjacent buildings, creating a concentrated risk zone
- All oil well spacings comply with standards (minimum 21.54m between wells)

## IV. Visual Information Supplementary Analysis

**Visual Context from Image Analysis:**

The satellite imagery reveals additional contextual factors that compound the identified risks:

1. **Facility Proximity Concerns:**
   - The orange-red building (ID 0) and smaller white building (ID 2) appear to be operational facilities (possibly power house, duty room, or equipment rooms) based on their construction and positioning
   - The white circular structure adjacent to buildings matches the detected tank (ID 1), confirming its identity as a storage tank

2. **Environmental Risk Factors:**
   - **Sparse vegetation and dry soil conditions** increase fire propagation risk
   - **Multiple access roads** indicate frequent vehicle traffic, potentially introducing ignition sources
   - **Open terrain** provides no natural firebreaks between the tank and buildings

3. **Operational Indicators:**
   - Vehicle tracks suggest active operations, increasing likelihood of equipment use near the tank
   - The presence of multiple oil wells indicates active production, requiring strict adherence to safety spacing

4. **Missing Safety Features:**
   - No visible fire isolation barriers or emergency access routes around the tank area
   - Absence of clear safety zones between the tank and adjacent buildings

## V. Improvement Recommendations

1. **Immediate Relocation of Storage Tank**
   - Move the storage tank (ID 1) to maintain minimum 20m distance from all buildings
   - Preferred location: Downwind side of buildings based on prevailing wind patterns
   - Reference: SY/T 5466-2013 Section 4.3.1.2 - Tank area should be positioned in the left-rear area of well site

2. **Establish Fire Safety Zone**
   - Create a 10m clear zone around the storage tank free of combustible materials
   - Install fire-resistant barriers between tank and nearest buildings
   - Reference: SY/T 5225-2019 Section 3.3.4 - Remote control stations and surrounding 10m area should be free of flammable, explosive, and corrosive items

3. **Implement Emergency Access Routes**
   - Construct dedicated emergency vehicle access paths around the tank area
   - Ensure minimum 2m pedestrian clearance around all critical equipment
   - Reference: SY/T 5225-2019 Section 3.1.4 - Maintain 2m+ pedestrian channels around equipment

4. **Install Fire Detection and Suppression Systems**
   - Deploy automatic fire detection sensors on buildings adjacent to tank
   - Install fixed fire suppression systems on storage tank
   - Reference: GB 50183-2004 Section 6.5.7 - Fire protection requirements for tank installations

5. **Conduct Comprehensive Site Reassessment**
   - Perform detailed wind pattern analysis for optimal equipment positioning
   - Verify all facility distances against SY/T 5466-2013 drilling site layout requirements
   - Establish regular remote monitoring protocol for ongoing compliance verification

## VI. Executive Summary

The oilfield well site exhibits critical fire safety violations with the storage tank positioned dangerously close to operational buildings at distances of 9.67m and 10.52m, violating SY/T 5225-2019 requirements for minimum 20m separation, necessitating immediate corrective action to prevent potential catastrophic fire incidents.
